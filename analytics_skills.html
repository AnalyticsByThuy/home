Which Analytics Skills Are in High Demand Today?

In order to answer the question, I came up with the notion of creating a common project and scraping data with Jupyter for later analysis (another in Power BI). My motive was to discover the reliance between roles and skills specified in LinkedIn's popular job search website. Two countries were carried out: Vietnam, my home country, and India known for its technical expertise.

Web scraping with Jupyter

Assuming a job-seeker on LinkedIn, I delved into two overarching role categories: Data Scientist and Data Analyst. My scheme is based on fetching a job description, returned by GET method to check skills in demand: Python, Power BI, Jupyter, SQL, Tableau, Excel.

Next, I explored the insights through two distinct avenues: continuing the analysis within Jupyter or exporting the database to Power BI.

Key Insights:

  • The analytics job market is experiencing a staggering 81.05% increase in the number of posts from the current week compared to the previous week. Notably, Vietnam has outshined India in this trend, with a remarkable upsurge in analytics job opportunities. Delhi has emerged as the city with the highest number of job postings (43.27%) in India, while Ho Chi Minh City takes the lead in Vietnam with 55.75%.

  • In the job market, possessing skills in Python, SQL, and Excel is crucial, particularly for entry-level and mid-senior positions. Additionally, visualization skills such as Power BI and Tableau are also preferred. They appeared in around 30 - 45% of job postings, indicating more chances of work opportunities. Specifically:

    • Data Analysts: SQL skills are highly sought with 89.25% of job postings emphasizing this requirement. Excel and Python follow closely, with 84.75% and 80.75% respectively, forming a strong skillset combination in this sector.
    • Data Scientists: On the other hand, Python is required most as an estimated 79.75% of job postings specify it as a must-have skill. Excel ranks second at 64.01%, followed by SQL at 54.50%.

  • Entry-level positions place a stronger emphasis on all skills, underscoring their importance for aspiring analytics professionals starting their careers.

It is evident that a strong command of these skills is vital for success in the analytics job market. Professionals aspiring to excel in the analytics field should focus on developing their proficiency in Python, SQL, and Excel, first of all, to remain competitive and capitalize on the growing opportunities in the job market.
